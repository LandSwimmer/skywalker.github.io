What is Photosynthesis?

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants make their own food. Using sunlight, plants convert water from the soil and carbon dioxide from the air into glucose, a type of sugar that provides energy for growth and survival. During this process, plants release oxygen into the air, which is essential for most living organisms. Photosynthesis mainly takes place in the leaves, inside structures called chloroplasts.
